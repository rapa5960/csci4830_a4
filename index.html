Why I picked CU

This is a short explanation/story on how I ended up attending the University of Colorado Boulder.

Military Brat

Since the day I was born, my dad has been in the United States Air Force. So, my mom, dad, and 3 other siblings would constantly move to different states every 1-3 years. In fact, during k-12, I attended 9 different schools. So, I never got connected to a state and I never grew up thinking, "I'm going to go to that college, because that is where I am from."

Image Credit: The National Interest

Family Oriented

Because of the constant moving, I never really developed friendships that lasted longer than the time we were stationed in that state. So my 5 best friends were always my Mom, Dad, 2 sisters, and little brother. My dad finally retired in 2012 in Colorado Springs. I was a junior in High School at the time, so I knew I wanted to go to college somewhere close to home. I started researching what schools in Colorado had a good engineering program. I was choosing between Colorado School of Mines and CU Boulder. Visiting Boulder's campus sold me, and that is how I came to attend CU. It was the best of both worlds, I got some independence being about 2 hours from home, but I could still always go home if I wanted too. Fast forward 4 years, and I am now a senior getting ready to graduate in May.
